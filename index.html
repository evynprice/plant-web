About Keanu

Keanu Leaves is a Cucumber plant (Cucumis sativus), a type of creeping vine that loves water, sunlight, and climbing! He is a part of the Cucurbitaceae family, shared by other garden vegetables such as zucchini, squash, pumpkin, and watermelon.

Keanu is over 9 weeks old and spends his time under a warm grow light in my apartment closet. There are 5 individual cucumber plants living in the pot!

Keanu's Story

Keanu Leaves has been through several ups and downs throughout the semester. Starting as a small packet of seeds, it took several weeks to get the plants to germinate. I tried multiple methods including soaking the seeds in a wet paper towel for several days before planting. Eventually, planting the seeds in a new pot with fresh soil was enough to give the seeds a good start. I was careful to not over water the plant, only watering when the soil was dry. This combined with the warm artifical light it received in my closet was enough to keep the plant alive and growing over the course of the semester.

One issue with growing the plant indoors under the grow light was that it did not grow as quickly as previous cucumber plants that I have grown in our family garden. Ultimately the stems and leaves were smaller, which worked out better in the limited space environment. If I was to grow cucumbers indoors again, I would make sure that they have ample sunlight in a large window.

Overall, growing cucumber plants has been a challenging but rewarding experience. Although my plants did not grow to maturity, I was able to associate much of the material that we learned in lecture such as root, steam, and leaf morphology to real-life examples. I plan to take the knowledge I have learned to continue to grow plants in our family garden.
